Octopus

Octopuses possess three hearts, one to pump blood through the gills and two to circulate blood throughout the body. Their blood is also blue due to the presence of copper-based haemocyanin, which transports oxygen.

  • Scientific Name: Octopus Vulgaris
  • Average Length: 1-3 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 12-24 months
  • Habitat: Tropical; Temperate

The common octopus, Octopus vulgaris, is a medium-sized cephalopod known for its intelligence and camouflage abilities. It has a warty body, eight arms with suckers, and can change color and texture to blend in with its environment.
